    Now on to the careers

    Medical Laboratory Science (MLS) is a field that studies the analysis of human fluids, tissues, and cells to aid in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases. Medical Laboratory Scientists, also known as Medical Technologists, play a vital role in healthcare by providing crucial information to doctors about a patient's health.

    Phlebotomy is the process of drawing blood, typically for diagnostic tests or blood donation, usually performed by phlebotomists, nurses, or technicians. The procedure, known as venipuncture, involves puncturing a vein, often in the arm, with a needle to collect blood.

    Epidemiology is the study of the distribution, patterns, and determinants of health and disease conditions in a defined population. It also involves applying this knowledge to prevent diseases and improve public health. Essentially, epidemiology examines why some people are more susceptible to certain diseases than others, and how these diseases spread through populations.

    Homeopathy is a therapeutic medical system, which is based on the observation that substances that are capable of causing diseases of the mind or body in healthy people can be used in the dilute form as remedies to treat the similar disorder in someone who is ill

    Data science is a multidisciplinary field that combines principles and practices from various disciplines like mathematics, statistics, computer science, and artificial intelligence to extract knowledge and insights from data. It involves using a wide range of techniques, including advanced analytics, machine learning, and statistical methods, to analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions.

    Software engineering is a broad discipline that involves the design, development, testing, and maintenance of software applications. It applies engineering principles to create reliable, user-friendly, and efficient software solutions. Software engineers work with various programming languages and tools to build software for diverse applications, including games, business applications, and operating systems.

    An author is a person who composes or creates a written work, particularly a book, article, or other literary piece. They are also considered the originator or creator of something, whether it's a plan, idea, or project. In legal terms, an author is the creator of an original work that has been published.
