Phlebotomy

All About Phlebotomy

Phlebotomy is the process of drawing blood, typically for diagnostic tests or blood donation, usually performed by phlebotomists, nurses, or technicians. The procedure, known as venipuncture, involves puncturing a vein, often in the arm, with a needle to collect blood.

I learnt about Phlebotomy last year, through my dad. I am not really interested in it but I do think that its interesting. You can use it as a stepping stone to get into nursing school. There is not much information about Phlebotomy in South Africa, its a very hidden career so I do believe that there is a good amount of job opportunities.

Fun Fact: Phlebotomists are nicknamed as the vampires of health.

As I said there is not much information about Phlebotomy in South Africa, so it is only offered in a few organisations as learnerships.

  • PathCare Academy: Offers a two-year learnership (Further Education and Training Certificate: Phlebotomy Techniques, NQF level 4) that combines theoretical learning with practical work experience
  • SAQA (South African Qualifications Authority): Provides information on the Phlebotomy Certificate Program, which is a part-time, two-semester program.
  • Ampath Pathology: Offers a two-year learnership (Further Education and Training Certificate: Phlebotomy Techniques, NQF level 4) that combines theoretical learning with practical work experience.

If you are interested, you can do more research and goodluck!
